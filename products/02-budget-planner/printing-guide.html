How to Print Your Budget Planner

Quick Start (4 Easy Steps)

1

Open the File

Open any of the 4 color theme HTML files in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge.

2

Print to PDF

Press Ctrl+P (Windows) or Cmd+P (Mac). Select "Save as PDF" as the destination.

3

Check Settings

Make sure "Background graphics" is checked. Set margins to "Default" and paper to "Letter".

4

Save & Print

Save the PDF, then print on your home printer or upload to a print service.

Important: You MUST check "Background graphics" (or "Background colors and images" in some browsers) for the colors and design to appear correctly.

Which Pages to Print Multiple Copies

  • Monthly Budget Worksheet — Print 12 copies (one per month)
  • Daily Expense Tracker — Print 12 copies (one per month)
  • Monthly Bill Calendar — Print 12 copies (one per month)
  • Monthly Money Review — Print 12 copies (one per month)
  • All other pages — Print 1 copy each

Paper Recommendations

  • Standard: Regular 20lb printer paper works great for everyday use
  • Premium: 32lb or 28lb paper for a thicker, more luxurious feel
  • Cover page: Print on cardstock (65lb) for a beautiful cover

Binding Ideas

  • 3-ring binder: Hole-punch pages and organize in a binder with divider tabs
  • Disc-bound system: Use a disc-bound punch for a lay-flat planner
  • Clipboard: Clip current month's pages to a clipboard for your desk
  • Stapled booklet: Staple together for a simple, portable option

4 Color Themes Included

  • Sage Green (budget-planner-sage.html) — Calming, natural feel
  • Dusty Blue (budget-planner-dusty-blue.html) — Serene, focused energy
  • Warm Neutral (budget-planner-neutral.html) — Elegant, timeless look
  • Lavender (budget-planner-lavender.html) — Soft, soothing vibes
Pro Tip: You can mix and match color themes! Print your cover in Sage, monthly budgets in Dusty Blue, and savings trackers in Lavender for a custom look.

Troubleshooting

  • Colors not showing? Make sure "Background graphics" is checked in print settings.
  • Pages cut off? Set margins to "Default" or "None" and paper size to "Letter".
  • Blank pages? Try a different browser. Chrome usually gives the best results.
  • Text too small? Make sure your browser zoom is set to 100% before printing.
